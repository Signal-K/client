## Headline / Subhead Variants

All use Syne Mono at `text-4xl md:text-5xl`. Subheads at `text-base text-muted-foreground`.
The micro label above reads `01 / REAL ASTRONOMY` in all variants.

---

**Option A — Direct, game-first**
> Headline: `Real science.`
> Headline line 2: `Your kind of game.`
> Subhead: Deploy telescopes. Classify real NASA data. Make actual discoveries.

*Shortest. Punchy. The existing metadata title already uses this — validates it's the right direction.*

---

**Option B — Mission framing**
> Headline: `Your station.`
> Headline line 2: `The universe's data.`
> Subhead: You're a citizen scientist now. Deploy structures, classify real signals, and help map the cosmos.

*Emphasises ownership and scale. "Station" ties to the game hub UX.*

---

**Option C — Action-first**
> Headline: `Deploy. Classify.`
> Headline line 2: `Discover.`
> Subhead: Real telescope data. Real exoplanet candidates. Real science — wrapped in a game you'll actually play.

*Verb-driven. Works well with the terminal/monospace aesthetic.*

---

**Option D — Tension/contrast**
> Headline: `This isn't a simulation.`
> Subhead: Star Sailors puts real NASA and TESS data in your hands. Play the game. Do the science.

*Single headline line. Bold claim up front. Risk: could be confusing without context.*

---

**Option E — Community angle**
> Headline: `Join the fleet.`
> Headline line 2: `Map the cosmos.`
> Subhead: Thousands of citizen scientists have already made discoveries. Your telescope is ready.

*Social proof baked into headline. Works if live-stat strip shows real numbers.*

---

### Recommendation

**Option A** for launch — shortest, matches existing SEO title, proven direction.
**Option C** as A/B test — strongest verb energy, suits monospace type treatment.
